  • Guide to Creating a Data Governance Program

    A scalable organizational framework designed to help businesses implement ethical and structured data governance practices.

    This framework was developed for the Data Ethics, Privacy, and Humans course as part of my Master of Science in Data Science program at Willamette University. It serves as a comprehensive roadmap for organizations of varying sizes and domains looking to establish an effective data governance strategy from the ground up.

    Co-authored as a collaborative project, the guide breaks down the data governance lifecycle into four distinct, actionable phases: Engagement (defining the 'why'), Strategy (aligning the 'what'), Architecture & Design (structuring the 'how'), and Implementation (putting governance into active practice). The resulting framework offers business stakeholders a clear path to managing data assets responsibly and ethically.

  • Influence of the Pacific Decadal Oscillation on Salmon Smolts

    A climate and ecological data study modeling the long-term correlation between the PDO index and regional salmon populations.

    This project was built for the Python for Data Science course during my graduate studies at Willamette University. The goal of the research was to investigate how large-scale, long-term climate variations—specifically the Pacific Decadal Oscillation (PDO)—impact the health and migration patterns of salmon smolts.

    As a solo analysis, I aggregated and processed historical climate metrics and environmental datasets. Utilizing Python libraries like Pandas for data manipulation and Seaborn for advanced data visualization, I constructed time-series models to isolate ecological trends. The resulting visualizations effectively translate complex climate indices into accessible insights to support ongoing regional conservation discussions.

  • Chivolo

    A kids' video app that features shows with racially-diverse main characters.

    Chivolo was created in 2019 as project for my Bloc (now Thinkful) Full-Stack Engineering bootcamp. The requirements for the project were to create a responsive app using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, JQuery, and a public API of my choice. I chose to use the YouTube Data API. This was a solo project which I completed with occasional help from a mentor.

    In 2019, my then-four-years-old stepdaughter's favorite app was the YouTube Kids app. I regularly reviewed my stepdaughter's viewing history on the app to see what kinds of content she had seen. I found that very few videos in the app featured characters of color. When characters of color were present, they were often secondary characters who merely supported the main character's story line.

    In response, I created a video app for kids, like YouTube Kids, with videos that star characters of color. I sourced content from the YouTube Data API to provide kids with videos that center BIPOC characters. I hope Chivolo acts as a complement to white-main-character-dominated video apps like YouTube Kids to help children form positive associations with people of all races.
